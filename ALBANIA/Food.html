Albanian Food

Albanian food features Mediterranean and Balkan influences, with a focus on fresh, seasonal ingredients, hearty flavors, and communal meals. Below are some of the most popular traditional dishes.

Byrek

Byrek is a traditional Albanian savory pastry made with thin layers of dough filled with various ingredients. It is one of the most beloved and iconic dishes in Albania. It is typically baked in a large round pan and cut into pieces.It can also be made as individual rolls or spirals. The dough is usually flaky and crisp, similar to filo pastry, though homemade versions can be slightly thicker and softer. Byrek can consist of multiple different fillings most common being byrek me djathÃ«(cheese), Byrek me spinaq(spinach and cheese) and byrek me mish(minced meat and onions) peoples favourite usually being byrek me mish. It is served warm with yogurt and it is usually eaten during lunch time.

Petulla

Petulla are a traditional Albanian food made with dough and fried in oil and can be eaten with various different things.It is one of the most popular things to have for breakfast in Albania. It is typically eaten with cheese, honey, nutella, jam and more, it can be eaten with sweet or savoury things which makes it a really flexible breakfast. They usually have a soft texture but they can also be crispy. The best part is that it is quick and easy to make and they usually come in the shape of dough balls, they are almost like doughnuts texture wise.

Qofte

Qofte are also a traditional Albanian food, however they can be found in almost every balkan country. Qofte consist of beef mince most of the time, however they can also be made with chicken or lamb mince. They are usually seasoned with herbs and spices like onion, garlic, mint, and parsley to provide a unique flavour to the meat. They are cooked in various ways, most commonly by frying, but can also be baked, grilled, or simmered in sauce. Qofte can be served as a main course or appetizer.
