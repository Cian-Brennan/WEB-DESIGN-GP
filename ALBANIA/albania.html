Overview of Albania

Albania is a country located in southeastern Europe on the Balkan Peninsula. It shares borders with Montenegro, Kosovo, North Macedonia, and Greece. Albania has beautiful coastlines along both the Adriatic and Ionian seas. The capital and largest city in Albania is Tirana; it is known for its high tourist attraction, vibrant energy, colourful architecture, and fascinating history.

Albania’s landscape is super diverse, featuring mountains, hills, and pristine beaches. The Albanian Alps in the north are known for their dramatic and stunning scenery and are a popular destination for hikers and tourists. Along the south coast, the Albanian Riviera is famous for its crystal-clear waters and seaside villages.

Albania’s natural environment contributes to its reputation as a destination of exceptional beauty. Modern Albania is known for its warm hospitality and growing tourism industry. In recent years, it has gained international attention as an emerging travel destination, attracting an estimated 10–12 million visitors each year. Tourists are drawn to its unspoiled nature, historical landmarks, and affordable prices compared to other Mediterranean destinations.

Fun Facts about Albania

Mountains and Lakes

Albania is characterized by diverse landscapes, including the Accursed Mountains and beautiful lakes like Lake Ohrid, shared with North Macedonia.

Bunkers Everywhere

During the rule of Enver Hoxha, Albania built around 173,000 bunkers across the country for defense during potential invasions.

Independence Day

Albania declared independence from the Ottoman Empire on November 28, 1912. This date is celebrated annually as Albania’s Independence Day.
